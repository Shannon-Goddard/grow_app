Why the 2026 Hemp Law Matters for Home Growers

The cannabis industry is about to face a seismic shift. Once the hemp law takes effect, the free flow of cannabis genetics across state borders ends. Growers will no longer be able to order seeds from out-of-state seed banks. The genetics available within your state's borders become your only legal options.

What Exactly Changed?

The law excludes seeds capable of producing >0.3% total THC (including THCA) from the hemp definition. Interstate transport now carries real federal risk. There’s a grace period until Nov 2026, but smart growers are prepping now.[1] Home growing isn’t banned—but access to genetics just got way more local.

FAQs About the New Hemp Law

Will I still be able to buy seeds online?

In-state yes. Interstate high-THC seeds become risky after Nov 2026. The CI Seed Map shows exactly what’s available legally where you are.[1]

When does it take effect?

Effective Nov 12, 2026[2]

What about seeds I already have?

You're good! Just don't try to take them across your states border.[3]

What about clones?

Same as a seed. It will no longer be about the current state not having THC, but what it will be when fully matured.[1]
