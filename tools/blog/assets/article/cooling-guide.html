Why Heat Hurts Your Plants

A hot tent’s like a sauna for your plants—too sweaty, and they get stressed. Over 85°F, buds shrink, leaves droop, and growth slows, kinda like you wilting in summer. Humidity’s gotta stay 40-60% too, or mold sneaks in. Keep it chill, and your plants party hard. [1]

I learned this the hard way—cooked my first grow ‘til I got a fan. Aim for 75-80°F in veg, 70-85°F in flower, and your buds’ll thank you, bruh.

Cooling 101: The Basics

Your tent’s like a little house—fans are breezy friends keeping it cool. An inline fan pulls hot air out, swapping it 4-6 times a minute. Clip-on fans inside stir the air so leaves don’t sweat. Open vents let fresh air in, and a hygrometer tracks temp and humidity. [2]

Room AC helps, but tents still run 10° hotter—crazy, right? For a 4x4 tent, a 6-inch inline fan (200-400 CFM) works. Smaller tents, try 4-inch. The tent sizing guide pairs fans with tents.

SoCal Cooling Hacks

Growing in SoCal’s wild—heat waves and power outages mess with grows. I run lights from 6pm to 6am for veg, or 6pm to 12pm for flower, when it’s cooler outside. From May to October? I pause growing—too hot, and California’s planned outages hit hard. [3]

Another hack: exhaust hot air at night, not noon. If power’s sketchy, keep a battery fan handy. SoCal taught me to plan around seasons—your buds deserve that chill vibe, bruh.

Newbie Cooling Checklist

New grower? Here’s your game plan, bruh:

  • Fan Size: 4-inch for 2x2 tents, 6-inch for 4x4—check CFM for your space.
  • Vents: Open bottom vents, duct exhaust high to pull heat out.
  • Temp Goal: 75-85°F, tweak with plant stage (cooler for flower).
  • Humidity: 40-60%, use a cheap hygrometer to stay on track.
  • Hacks: Frozen bottles near fans, damp towels for humidity, night exhaust.
The MyGrow feature in the app logs your tent’s temp and humidity daily—keeps it dialed in! [4]

Your Questions, Answered

Why’s my tent so hot?

Lights crank heat—add an inline fan to swap air 4-6 times a minute. [2]

Can I cool without AC?

Yep, fans and night lights work—frozen bottles help too, bruh. [4]

What’s the best temp for buds?

75-80°F for veg, 70-85°F for flower—keeps ‘em happy. [1]
